Glass Classification

About

Glass has many varied applications in various industries. Depending on the various properties like refractive index, chemical composition etc. the glass is classified into various categories. These categories signify the utility for which the given glass is suitable. For example, glass with almost null values of Mg, Ba and Fe (magnesium, barium and iron) are suitable for containers, etc.

Given below are the types of glass being classified in the following project.

Building windows float processed

Float glass is a sheet of glass made by floating molten glass on a bed of molten metal, typically tin, although lead and other various low-melting-point alloys were used in the past. This method gives the sheet uniform thickness and very flat surfaces. Modern building windows are made from float glass since the process of floating makes the windows stronger. 

 

            

          
 
        


        

          

            
            

              
Building windows non-float processed

              
In the smaller houses where there is no need for industrial strong windows, non float type windows are sufficient. This type of window is more malleable and can be made to be more decorative and aesthetic. 
                  
                    





                    
   

            

          
 
        


        

          

            
            

              
Vehicle windows float processed

              
Float glass is a sheet of glass made by floating molten glass on a bed of molten metal, typically tin, although lead and other various low-melting-point alloys were used in the past. This method gives the sheet uniform thickness and very flat surfaces. 
                  Since float processed windows are stronger than its non-float processed counterparts, they are used in making windows for aeroplanes, helicopters etc                   
                  

            

          
 
        


      


      


        

            

              
              

                
Containers

                
The glass material of containers is usually transparent and thick but not as thick as building windows. It is sturdier than the tableware glass material.                   
                    
 

              

            
 
          


          

            

              
              

                
Tableware

                
All the tableware material like glasses, plates etc. come in this category. The glass is more malleable and can be easily moulded into decorative shapes. Color can also be added to make it more aesthetic.                    
                    

              

            
 
          


          

            

              
              

                
Headlamps

                
Headlamps of cars, bikes etc. are made from these types of glass. They should have a specific refractive index so as to disperse light correctly in appropriate directions.
